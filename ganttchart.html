Working draft for discussion

HCB x MetLife Preliminary Project Plan

Relative Gantt plan for a cross-border claims support engagement. Week 0 is assumed to be Trustee / SOW approval. The timeline is deliberately conservative and allows for the proposal assumption that set-up, access, compliance and onboarding may take approximately three to six months before steady-state delivery.

Planning basisRelative weeks from approval
Total view52-week engagement horizon
Critical pathScope + security + VDI + QA calibration
Key controlReporting consistency across geographies

Executive view

This plan converts the email themes into workstreams: pre-mobilisation, mobilisation, delivery and transition. It also elevates reporting and QA calibration as a workstream because consistent recommendations, quality scoring and dashboard outputs will be the central control mechanism across Australia, the United States, the United Kingdom and South Africa.

  • Use this as a discussion baseline, not a committed project schedule.
  • Dates should only be added after MetLife confirms scope, volumes, SLAs, system access lead times and reporting audiences.
  • The go-live decision should be gated by scope sign-off, contracting, security, VDI access, training, QA calibration and dashboard readiness.
Reporting design principle:
Reporting is not only a dashboard output. It is the governance control that proves whether delivery, quality, recommendations, exceptions and escalation are consistent across geographies while still allowing for country-specific differences.
